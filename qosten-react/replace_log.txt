        Text: Why is the President of Bangladesh called formal head? Explain.
        Answer: The President is called the formal head of Bangladesh because he holds the highest constitutional position but does not have direct executive powers.\n
In the parliamentary democratic system of Bangladesh, the President is the ceremonial head of state. All government functions are conducted in his name, but the actual executive power lies with the Prime Minister and the Cabinet. According to the Constitution, the President is the head of the state, but he exercises his duties on the advice of the Prime Minister. That is why he is called a formal or ceremonial head.

Maintains law and order in the district\n

  Answer: 

  Parts:
    [1] Label: A, Marks: 1
        Text: What is local administration?
        Answer: Local administration is the governance system at the local level, including divisions, districts, and upazilas.
    [2] Label: B, Marks: 2
        Text: Why is secretariat called the nerve in the administrative system? Explain.
        Answer: Bangladesh Secretariat is called the nerve center of the administrative system because:\n
It is the focal point where all major administrative decisions of the country are made
The Secretariat consists of several Ministries, each headed by a Minister
The executive and administrative powers of each ministry are vested in the Secretary
All decisions flow from the Secretariat to lower levels of administration\n
Just like nerves carry signals from the brain to the body, the Secretariat sends decisions and policies to all parts of the government. That is why it is called the nerve of the administrative system.
    [3] Label: C, Marks: 3
        Text: Which department does Mr. Azmol serve in? Explain.
        Answer: Mr. Azmol serves in the Judiciary Department.\n
The Judiciary is one of the three departments of the government of Bangladesh. Its main functions are:\n
To establish justice in society
To preserve the individual freedom and fundamental rights of citizens
To protect the rights of the people independently and impartially\n
Why is an independent Judiciary important?
Implementing the rule of law is essential to establish equal rights and good governance for all citizens
It is the responsibility of the Judiciary to ensure justice according to the law
The Judiciary protects the fundamental rights of citizens mentioned in the Constitution\n
What happens if the Judiciary is not independent?
The state administration can deprive people of their fundamental rights
Influential criminals can escape the law even after committing serious crimes
People's confidence in the law is reduced and chaos is created in society\n
Hence, ensuring the rule of law through an independent Judiciary is essential for establishing good governance. Transparency and accountability of administration cannot be ensured without the rule of law.
    [4] Label: D, Marks: 4
        Text: The department of Mr. Arman controls the department of Mr. Rafiq. Analyze.
        Answer: First, let's identify the departments:
Mr. Arman is an administrative head of a district
→
→ He works in the Executive Department (District Administration)
Mr. Rafiq is elected from 'Ka' constituency and serves people's interests
→
→ He works in the Legislature Department (Jatiya Sangsad)\n
Does the Executive control the Legislature? No, the statement is incorrect. Actually, the Legislature controls the Executive.\n
How does the Legislature control the Executive?
The Legislature maintains control by asking questions in Parliament about the Administration's work
It checks the quality and rationality of the Administration's decisions
The Prime Minister, Cabinet, and Executive Department are accountable to the Legislature for all administrative matters\n
Tools of Legislative control:
Adjournment motions
Censure motions
Questions to ministers
No-confidence motions against the Prime Minister or ministers\n
If the Legislature loses confidence in the government, the cabinet has to resign.\n
Conclusion: In a democratic state, the Legislature Department exercises control over the Executive Department through accountability, not the other way around. Therefore, the statement that Mr. Arman's department controls Mr. Rafiq's department is not correct.
